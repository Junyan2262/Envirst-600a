Objective of Research

Wind energy projects on Native lands can strengthen Native energy sovereignty and independence by giving tribes control over their own energy resources and reducing dependence on outside suppliers. These projects can create jobs, generate revenue, and allow tribes to reinvest in their communities and support areas such as education and health care. By forgoing fossil fuels, wind energy reduces environmental harm. This self-reliance gives tribes the ability to make decisions that reflect their priorities and build long-term economic resilience. We performed cost benefit analysis of implementing wind energy on Native lands. This can be used to help Native people get support to implement wind energy. We looked specifically at a case study of a tribe that implemented a wind energy project and evaluate this project.
