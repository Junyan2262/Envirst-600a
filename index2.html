Map of Lower Sioux Indian Community

The project site is the Community’s Reservation, located in Redwood County, Minnesota (the Site), along the southern bank of the Minnesota River south of the city of Morton (the City). The reservation is 1,743 acres in size. Figure 1-1 shows the Site. The Community has a number of enterprises, including the Jackpot Junction Casino Hotel (the Casino). The Casino complex also houses the Dacotah Exposition Center and Outdoor Amphitheater, and the Lower Sioux Lodge.

Lower Sioux Indian Tribe Advantages

● The Lower Sioux Indian Tribe has many advantages that allowed them to pursue this project

● Aid and funding of the government

● Businesses include: Jackpot Junction Casino Hotel, The Dacotah Exposition Center and Outdoor Amphitheater, and the Lower Sioux Lodge

● Not all Tribes have these same opportunities

● Tribes find wind power development nearly impossible if they lack any particular advantages—no participation in CERT (Council of Energy Resource Tribes), no fossil fuel economy, no federal grants for wind power development, etc.

Lower Sioux Indian Community Case Study

Goals:

● Aim to provide clean, renewable energy to the Lower Sioux reservation through the installation of a 1.5 MW wind turbine and an accompanying solar field.

● Sell surplus energy to nearby utility grids.

Benefits:

● Decrease dependency on non-renewable energy.

● Create employment and economic opportunities.
